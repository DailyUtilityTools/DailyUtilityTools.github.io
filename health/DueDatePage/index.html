Unlike math class, here's what that means

Pregnancy is technically 40 weeks, but you aren't actually pregnant for the first 2 weeks (before ovulation). So "Week 1" of pregnancy is actually your period. (Confusing, we know).

Example Calculation

LMP: Jan 1st.

  1. Add 7 Days: Jan 8th.
  2. Subtract 3 Months: Oct 8th.
  3. Add 1 Year: October 8th. (Due Date).

Expert Insight

The "4th Trimester": The first 3 months after birth are crucial. The baby is technically still a fetus (useless outside the womb) and needs constant holding. Don't plan to "bounce back" instantly.

Why this matters

Knowing your due date helps you book scans, schedule work leave, and mentally prepare for sleepless nights.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I don't know my LMP? Doctors use an early Ultrasound to measure the "Crown Rump Length" (CRL) of the baby. It's the most accurate way to date a pregnancy.

Can I choose my due date? No. But a C-section or induction can be scheduled around 39 weeks.

Is 42 weeks safe? Doctors usually induce labor by 41-42 weeks because the placenta starts to degrade and risks increase.
