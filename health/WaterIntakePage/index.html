Did You Know? ðŸ’¡

  • Thirst Lag: By the time you feel thirsty, you are already 1-2% dehydrated. Drink before you are dry.
  • Food Water: About 20% of your water intake comes from food (fruits, veggies, soup). Cucumber is 96% water.
  • Over-hydration: Drinking too much water (Hyponatremia) can dilute your blood sodium and be fatal. Balance is key.

Expert Insight

The "Pee Test": The easiest way to check hydration? Check the color.

  • Clear: Too much water.
  • Pale Straw: Perfect.
  • Yellow/Apple Juice: Drink water NOW.
  • Dark Orange: Go to a doctor.

Why this matters

Dehydration causes headaches, fatigue, and "brain fog." If you feel tired at 2 PM, drink water before you brew more coffee.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does coffee count? Yes. Caffeine is a mild diuretic (makes you pee), but you retain most of the fluid. It counts towards your total, just don't make it 100% of your intake.

What about electrolytes? If you sweat for >1 hour, drink sports drinks or add salt to your water. Plain water won't replace the salt you lost.

Cold vs Warm water? Cold water absorbs slightly faster and burns a few extra calories. Warm water is better for digestion. Drink whatever you like.
