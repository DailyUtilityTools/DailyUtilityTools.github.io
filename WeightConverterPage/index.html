Understanding weight vs mass

Mass

Mass is the amount of matter in an object. It remains constant regardless of location. Measured in kilograms or grams in the metric system.

Weight

Weight is the force exerted by gravity on a mass. It changes based on gravitational pull. On the Moon, your weight would be about 1/6 of your Earth weight, but your mass stays the same.

Everyday Usage

In daily conversation, we often say "weight" when technically referring to "mass." Our converter handles both terms as they're commonly used interchangeably on Earth.

Weight measurement systems

Metric System (SI Units)

Kilogram (kg)

The base unit of mass. Used for body weight, food, and most everyday items.

Gram (g)

Used for smaller measurements - ingredients, medications, jewelry. One thousandth of a kilogram.

Milligram (mg)

Used for precision measurements - medicine dosages, vitamins. One thousandth of a gram.

Metric Ton (tonne)

Used for large quantities - vehicles, cargo, building materials. Equal to 1,000 kilograms.

Imperial System

Pound (lb)

Primary weight unit in the US. Used for body weight, food, and general measurements.

Ounce (oz)

Smaller imperial unit. Used for recipes, postal rates, and package weights.

Ton (US ton)

Used for large weights - vehicles, equipment. Equal to 2,000 pounds (not the same as metric ton).

What's the difference between a metric ton and a US ton?

A metric ton (tonne) equals 1,000 kg or 2,204.6 pounds. A US ton equals 2,000 pounds or 907.2 kg. The metric ton is slightly heavier.

How many ounces are in a pound?

There are 16 ounces in one pound. This is a defined conversion in the imperial system.

Why do nutrition labels show both grams and ounces?

The US requires food labels to show both metric (grams) and imperial (ounces) measurements to accommodate different user preferences and international standards.

How much does a liter of water weigh?

One liter of water weighs approximately 1 kilogram or 2.205 pounds at standard temperature and pressure. This makes metric conversions convenient.

What's a stone in weight?

A stone is a British imperial unit equal to 14 pounds or about 6.35 kilograms. It's commonly used in the UK for body weight.

Are grams and ounces the same?

Expert Insights

Goodbye "Le Grand K"

For 130 years, the kilogram was defined by a physical cylinder of platinum-iridium locked in a vault in Paris ("Le Grand K"). In 2019, scientists redefined it based on Planck's constant, a fundamental constant of quantum physics. Now the kilogram is defined by the universe itself, not a hunk of metal!

Mass vs. Weight

Your Mass is the same everywhere. Your Weight changes with gravity.

  • Earth: 100 kg mass weighs 220 lbs.
  • Mars: 100 kg mass weighs 83 lbs.
  • Space: 100 kg mass weighs 0 lbs (but still has inertia!).
