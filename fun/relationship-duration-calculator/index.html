Relationship Duration Calculator

Trying to remember if your anniversary is next week or next month? Or just want to brag about how many seconds you've tolerated your partner? This calculator counts every heartbeat.

What this calculator does

  • Precision Tracking: Years, Months, Days, Hours, Minutes, Seconds.
  • Heartbeat Counter: A romantic (or slightly creepy) count of how many times your hearts have beaten since you met.
  • Milestone Alerts: Tells you when to book dinner to avoid an argument.

The Formula

  • Heartbeats: Average human heart rate is ~70-80 bpm. We multiply the minutes by 150 (combined) to get your "Love Pulse."

Unlike Instagram captions, here's what that means

Did You Know?

  • The 7-Year Itch: Statistics show divorce rates spike around year 7. If you make it to year 8, you're statistically safer.
  • Syncing Up: Couples who live together often synchronize their heart rates and breathing patterns during sleep. Biology is romantic.
  • Memory Lane: The "Peak-End Rule" suggests we remember relationships by their most intense moments and how they ended, not the duration. Make the intense moments good ones.

Expert Insight

The "2-2-2 Rule": To keep the duration going, try this:

  • Every 2 weeks, go on a date.
  • Every 2 months, go for a weekend away.
  • Every 2 years, take a week-long vacation. It breaks the routine that kills long-term romance.

Why this matters

Because "about 3 years" sounds less impressive than "1,095 days of commitment." Also, if you forget your anniversary, this page might save your life.
