Expert Insight

Inch-Pounds vs Foot-Pounds: Be careful! 1 Foot-Pound = 12 Inch-Pounds. If a spec asks for 100 in-lbs and you use 100 ft-lbs, you will snap the bolt instantly. (You applied 12x the force!).

Why this matters

Overtightening bolts strips threads or cracks parts (oil pans, carbon fiber bike frames). Undertightening causes parts to rattle loose. Accuracy prevents expensive repairs.
