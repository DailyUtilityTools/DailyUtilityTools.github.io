  • A "Jiffy": It's not just a phrase. In physics, a "jiffy" is the time it takes light to travel one centimeter (about 33 picoseconds).

Expert Insight

Precision Matters: When converting, be careful with "Significant Figures." If you measure a room as "10 feet" (rough guess), don't convert it to "3.048 meters" (super precise). It implies accuracy you don't have. Just say "3 meters."

  • Cooking Tip: Professional bakers weigh flour in grams, not "cups." a cup of flour can weigh anywhere from 120g to 150g depending on how packed it is. Measurements by weight are always strictly accurate.

Why this matters

Whether you're following a French recipe, buying furniture from IKEA (European sizes!), or answering a physics problem, using the wrong unit can lead to salty cakes, furniture that doesn't fit, or crashed satellites.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the US use Imperial? Stubbornness and cost. The US Congress authorized the Metric system in 1866, but never made it mandatory. Changing every road sign and factory tool in America would cost billions.

What is a "Metric Ton"? A Metric Ton (tonne) is 1,000 kg (~2,204 lbs). A US Short Ton is 2,000 lbs. A UK Long Ton is 2,240 lbs. Yes, it's confusing. Always clarify which "ton" you mean.

Is a Gallon the same everywhere? No! A US Gallon (3.78 L) is smaller than a UK Imperial Gallon (4.54 L). This is why UK cars seem to get better MPGâ€”their gallons are bigger!
