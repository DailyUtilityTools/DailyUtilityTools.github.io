Unlike math class, here's what that means

Inflation is the silent tax.

  • Average Rate: ~3% per year.
  • Rule of 72: At 3%, prices double every 24 years.
  • Your Raise: If you didn't get a 3% raise this year, you technically took a pay cut.

Expert Insight

Assets vs Cash: Hard assets (Real Estate, Gold, Stocks) generally rise with inflation. Cash loses value. This is why rich people own things and poor people save cash.

Why this matters

If you are saving for a goal 20 years away, you need to account for inflation. $1 Million sounds like a lot, but in 30 years, it might only buy what $400k buys today.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is deflation good? No. If prices drop, people stop spending (waiting for lower prices), companies fire workers, and the economy crashes (The Great Depression). The Fed targets 2% inflation as the "sweet spot."

How is CPI calculated? Top economists track a "basket of goods" (milk, gas, rent, clothes) and see how the total price changes month to month.
