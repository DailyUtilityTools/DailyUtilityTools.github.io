Did You Know? ðŸ’¡

  • A "Cup" isn't a Cup: Your coffee mug is not a measurement. A standard US Cup is 237ml. A Metric Cup (used in Australia, Canada) is 250ml. If you use the wrong one in baking, your cake might flop!
  • The "Pint" Trap: "A pint's a pound the world around" is a lie.
    • US Pint: 16 fl oz (473 ml).
    • UK Imperial Pint: 20 fl oz (568 ml).
    • This is why beer is bigger in London!
  • Oil Barrels: Crude oil is measured in "barrels" (bbl). One barrel is exactly 42 US gallons.

Expert Insight

The Meniscus: When measuring liquids in a glass cylinder, the surface curves (usually dips in the middle). To be accurate, always measure from the bottom of the curve (the meniscus), not the edges.

  • Baking Tip: Wet and Dry measuring cups are different! A liquid cup has a spout and space at the top. A dry cup is flat so you can level it off. Using a liquid cup for flour usually results in too much flour.

Why this matters

Chemistry and Baking are the same thing: chemistry you can eat. Both require exact ratios. Too much liquid makes specific chemical reactions fail (leaving you with a goopy brownie or an exploded lab).

Frequently Asked Questions

How many tablespoons in a cup? 16 Tablespoons = 1 Cup.

Is a Fluid Ounce the same as an Ounce? No!

  • Fluid Ounce: Volume (Space).
  • Ounce: Weight (Mass).
  • Exception: 1 fl oz of water weighs roughly 1 oz. But 1 fl oz of honey weighs 1.5 oz.

How many Liters in a Gallon? 3.785 Liters in a US Gallon.

What is a "cc"? Cubic Centimeter. It is exactly the same as a Milliliter (ml). Doctors use "cc" (e.g., "Give him 10cc of epi!") because it sounds cool, but it's just 10ml.
